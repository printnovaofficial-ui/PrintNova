Find the Perfect Filament for Your Own

Choose the right material before you print.

This guide helps customers understand the tradeoffs between printability, flexibility, surface finish, strength, and heat resistance before placing an order.

Polylactic Acid Filament (PLA)

Easy, clean, and cost-effective.

  • Speciality: very easy to print, low warping, biodegradable, smooth surface finish, cheap and widely available
  • Uses: prototypes, decorative items, toys, models and miniatures, educational prints
  • Notes: not very heat resistant and can become soft around 60°C
Thermoplastic Polyurethane Filament (TPU)

Flexible and impact-resistant.

  • Speciality: flexible, rubber-like, durable, good vibration absorption, resistant to oils and grease
  • Uses: phone cases, gaskets and seals, shoe soles, RC car tires, protective covers
  • Notes: harder to print than PLA and requires slow print speed
Carbon Fiber Reinforced PLA

Lightweight with a matte professional finish.

  • Speciality: very stiff and strong, lightweight, matte finish, less visible layer lines, better dimensional stability than PLA
  • Uses: drone frames, mechanical parts, RC components, camera mounts, structural prototypes
  • Notes: abrasive and requires a hardened steel nozzle
Carbon Fiber Reinforced Nylon

Strong, heat-resistant, and functional.

  • Speciality: extremely strong, high heat resistance, durable, wear resistant, lightweight but tough
  • Uses: functional mechanical parts, gears, automotive components, industrial tools, load-bearing parts
  • Notes: difficult to print and requires high temperature plus dry filament
